# Summons with Notice

Summons with Notice (UD-1) is used to commence a matrimonial action in New York Supreme Court when the plaintiff serves a summons with notice instead of a summons and complaint together.

## Form details

- **Form Number:** UD-1
- **Case Type:** Divorce, Divorce - Uncontested
- **Legal Action:** Divorce, Uncontested Divorce

## When you may need this form

Plaintiffs typically file UD-1 at the start of an uncontested or contested divorce when beginning the case in Supreme Court. The form notifies the defendant that an action has been started and identifies the relief sought.

## What to prepare before filling out this form

- **Plaintiff name** — full legal name of the person starting the case
- **Defendant name** — full legal name of the spouse
- **County** — NYC borough where you will file (Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, Bronx, or Staten Island)
- **Court** — New York Supreme Court in that county
- **Relief requested** — what you are asking the court to grant in the divorce
